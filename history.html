Our Story

A journey of passion, preservation, and creating meaningful connections with nature.

From Dream to Reality

Lake House began as a shared vision between two nature lovers who dreamed of creating a sanctuary where modern comfort meets pristine wilderness. Our journey spans over a decade of careful planning, sustainable building, and deep respect for the natural world.

  1. 2012

    The Discovery

    Erik and Maja first discovered this hidden gem on the shores of a pristine Norwegian lake. The untouched forest, crystal-clear water, and abundant wildlife captured their hearts immediately. They spent months camping here, studying the ecosystem, and envisioning a retreat that would honor this sacred space.

  2. 2014

    Sustainable Planning

    Working with environmental architects and local conservationists, we developed plans that would integrate seamlessly with the existing landscape. Every tree was mapped, every animal path respected. Our commitment: not a single mature tree would be removed during construction.

  3. 2016

    Mindful Construction

    Construction began using locally sourced materials and traditional Norwegian building techniques. Solar panels were installed, rainwater collection systems put in place, and a state-of-the-art wastewater treatment system designed to protect the lake's pristine waters.

  4. 2018

    First Guests

    Lake House welcomed its first guests ‚Äî a couple seeking respite from city life. Their tears of joy upon seeing the sunrise over the lake confirmed we had created something truly special. Word spread organically through heartfelt recommendations.

  5. 2024

    A Living Legacy

    Today, Lake House stands as a testament to sustainable hospitality. Our organic garden feeds guests and local wildlife alike. The forest has thrived, welcoming new species. We've hosted over 500 guests, each leaving with renewed connection to nature and themselves.

"We wanted to create more than just accommodation. We dreamed of a place where people could remember what it feels like to be truly alive, surrounded by the wisdom and beauty of the natural world."
